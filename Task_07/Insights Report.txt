Trends and Observations:
Most users spend around 6–10 hours daily on screens.
Higher focus sessions and deep work hours improve productivity.
Higher stress, burnout risk, and emotional exhaustion reduce productivity.
Productivity decreases slightly for users with extremely high screen time.
Stress levels are moderately distributed among users.

Possible Issues in the Dataset:
Some columns had missing values and were filled using mean/mode methods.
Label encoding converts categories into numbers, which may not represent actual relationships.
The dataset is very large (5 million rows), causing:
high memory usage,
crowded visualizations,
slower processing.
Scatter plots contain overlapping points, making patterns harder to see.

Key Conclusions:
Balanced digital usage improves productivity.
Deep work and concentration positively affect performance.
Excessive screen time and burnout negatively impact efficiency.
Mental health factors play an important role in workplace productivity.
